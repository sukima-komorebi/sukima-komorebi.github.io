About Origami

Origami(折り紙) is a Japanes word meaning "folding paper". The "ori(折り) means "fold", and "gami(紙） means paper in Japanese. Today, I will be showing you the steps on how to make a origami crane, whch is a relatively simeple type of origami.

Steps on How to Fold an Origami Crane

Step 1:

You need to find a square piece of origami paper. It could be any size, but I reccomend a 15cm x 15cm because it is easy to work on.

Step 2:

Fold the paper in half. Make sure the colored side of the paper is at the back, when you fold. two corners should be folded right on top of each other.

Step 3:

Fold the paper in half again, forming a triangle.

Step 4:

Pull out one flap of the paper, so it forms a square.

Step 5:

...as so.

Step 6:

Flip over the paper.

Step 7:

do the same thing you did in step 4 on the other side of the paper. It should form a square.

Step 8:

Fold in one flap of the paper so that it lines up with the middle crease in the original square. When you do this, make sure the corner with several flaps are placed atthe bottom, closest to you. Do this for both the right and left side.

Step 9:

After you made the crease in step 8, fold it back out so it forms a square again. Then, fold the top corner down. When you do this, make sure the tip lines up with the middle crease, and the two non-right corners are at the end of the creases we made in step 8.

Step 10:

Fold back the corner from step 9 after you made a crease. Pull one flap of the very bottom corner all the way up.

Step 11:

...like so. Make sure you do steps 8-10 again foor the back side.

Step 12:

Fold in the two sides so that it lines up with the straight line in the middle. When you do this, make sure the flappy side of the paper is at the top, furthest from you.

Step 13:

...like so. Make sure do step 12 again for the back side.

Step 14:

Take one flap from one side of the paper, and fold it the other direction. Do the same thing on the back side as well.

Step 15:

Pull up the bottom part of the paper all of the way up, so that it matches the height of the two flaps at the top. When you do this, make sure the coner with two separate "legs" with a vertical gap in between is at the top, furthest from you.

Step 16:

Fold one flap from one side of the paper the other way. Repeat that for the back side.

Step 17:

Then, fold down one flap from each side of thecrane to make the wings.

Step 18:

Fold down one corner that is sticking up, to make the face.

Completed!!

And now you've got it! A simple origami crane!
